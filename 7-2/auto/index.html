As a general technology trend in the future, autonomous driving changes not only the way people travel, but also the way people live. It involves a wide range of areas: Internet of Things technology, communication technology, artificial intelligence, law, automotive vehicle operating systems and software, urban planning, and more.
Autonomous vehicles are no longer simply viewed as a means of transportation, but as a dynamic mobile space, as new type of architecture of city. People’s activities in the car have also become more diverse, and the application fields of vehicles are also more extensive. In this project, we focus on the way people interact with autonomous vehicles in the future. A new visual language will be designed in this project.

Which Information Make Sense when Everyone Becomes a Passenger?

From the driver to the passenger, the identity of the people because of automous driving will change. And this change means that we need to find ways to communicate differently from the current form, as well as the way information is provided. We create an information matrix by studying the user experience map and the potential needs of the user. From this information matrix, we can determine which information is more important and what information channels the car can use to transmit information to users. In the future use scenarios, urban space must be dynamic. In this dynamic scenario, how can the car effectively transmit information. In this case, we believe that dynamic visual language can play an important role.

Which Visual Language can Transmit Information in a Dynamic Environment?

To explore the visual language, we first created a systematic sketch matrix. We have arranged and combined each item to spread out as many possible actions as possible. Since the visual language we design is dynamic, not a rigid icon, we need to use other methods to “translate” the actions in our sketch matrix. We try to use our body language to interpret different actions to determine whether this action is in line with people’s daily perceptions and to filter the actions in the Matrix.Â 

Next, LED strips are used as our display medium to simulate the actions we selected. A short video was also produced as a result of the first phase. After that we combined the previous research and improved the results.
