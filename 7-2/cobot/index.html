The smart factory proposed by Industry 4.0 also mentions that people and robots work together in a more harmonious way. To do this, the robot must have a deep understanding of the environment around it, as well as the working conditions of other robots.

Once the isolation of the space between the person and the robot disappears, the interaction between the person and the robot will become more frequent and close. The relationship between people and robots will change accordingly. The robot is no longer a simple workplace, but an assistant. In this project we study how people should interact with robots in the future.

What is Collaborative Robot?

Flexibility

The flexibility of collaborative robots is mainly reflected in the flexibility of programming. Today’s desktop collaborative robots can be programmed by recording tracks. Collaborative robots can reduce the cost of line retrofits. This is very attractive for small and medium-sized businesses.

Safety

Collaborative robots are equipped with a large number of sensors.Â  The robot can know whether the person has carried out dangerous operations through the sensor, and thus fully guarantees the safety of people and the safety of production.

Efficiency

The working characteristics of robots are often repetitive, fast, and high-intensity, while people are good at fuzzy cognition.Â  The efficiency of human-robot collaboration is much higher than the efficiency of people and robots working separately.

In this project, our focus is on large or medium-sized robotic arms for industrial use. Large robotic arms are several times faster and more powerful than small robotic arms, and the hazard is higher than that of small robots. In order to cooperate with such industrial robots, the transmission of data must be fast, accurate and without delay. The collaboration must be safe and user-friendly

Design - Re-division of the Work Area

Identification area

Only robots are allowed to work in this area. Staff are not allowed to enter. If a worker enters this area, the robot will cease to operate.

Â 

Collaboration Area

Only in this area, the worker’s command to the robot will be recognized, and the robot movement will be slowed down accordingly.

Robot Work Area

The identity of the person will be recognized here. The robot will know if he is a staff member and how many workers there are.

Â 

Human-centered collaboration

The motion pattern of the robot is difficult to understand. So we designed a human-centered collaboration model. When the worker interacts with the robot in the collaboration area, the worker should be the center of the interactive system. Worker is considered to be the reference coordinate of this system. When a person gives a “forward” command, the robot moves forward according to the worker’s position.
